Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about Suno AI, Udio, and AI music creation

How to add guitar solo in Suno AI?

+

Insert the [Guitar Solo] meta tag as a standalone section in your lyrics — typically between [Verse 2] and [Chorus]. This tells Suno AI to generate an instrumental guitar break at that exact spot. The tag works for any genre but produces the best results in rock, metal, and synthwave. Combine with instrumental tags like "Electric Guitar Solo" in your style prompt for enhanced results.

What are the Suno AI meta tags for song structure?

+

The complete Suno meta tags list includes: [Intro] for opening sections (2-4 lines), [Verse 1] and [Verse 2] for narrative verses (4-6 lines each), [Pre-Chorus] to build tension (2-4 lines), [Chorus] for the main hook (4-6 lines), [Bridge] for contrast (4 lines), [Guitar Solo] for instrumental breaks (standalone), [Vocal Break] for a capella sections (2-4 lines), and [Outro] for the ending (2-4 lines). Each tag instructs Suno on how to musically interpret that section.

How to add background vocals / harmonies in Suno?

+

Wrap any lyric line in parentheses to mark it as a background vocal or harmony. Example: (I'm singing in the rain) instead of I'm singing in the rain. Suno will layer that line behind the main vocal as a backing track. This works for call-and-response, layered harmonies, and doubling effects. Use on key lines in verses or throughout the chorus for richer vocal texture.

What is the Suno style prompt character limit?

+

Suno AI enforces a strict 200-character limit on style prompts. You must use comma-separated tags instead of full sentences. If you exceed 200 characters, Suno silently truncates your prompt, potentially removing important tags from the end. Always use a character counter when building prompts. The optimal formula for 2026: Genre + Vibe + Vocals + Instruments + Production.

How to structure lyrics for the best Suno results?

+

Structure with 4-6 lines per section: [Intro] (2-4 lines) → [Verse 1] (4-6 lines) → optionally [Pre-Chorus] (2-4 lines) → [Chorus] (4-6 lines) → [Verse 2] → [Chorus] → [Bridge] (4 lines) → [Outro] (2-4 lines). Always place your strongest lyrics in the [Chorus] — this is the focal point. Use parentheses for harmonies and backing vocals. Avoid sections longer than 6 lines which can cause the AI to lose direction.

What is the Udio v4 prompt structure?

+

Udio v4 uses the same tag-stacking approach as Suno: comma-separated keywords across Genre, Vibe, Vocals, Instruments, and Production categories. However, Udio excels with production-heavy prompts and handles complex combinations better. Tags like Sidechain Compression, Glitch Effects, and Analog Warmth produce more noticeable results in Udio. The lyrics structure (meta tags) is identical between both platforms.

Why does my AI music sound clunky or drift off track?

+

The 6 most common causes: (1) Overloading the style prompt with conflicting tags — stick to 3-5 complementary choices. (2) Not using meta tags in lyrics — always wrap sections in [Verse]/[Chorus]/etc. (3) Exceeding the 200-character limit — Suno truncates silently. (4) Missing backing vocals — songs sound flat without harmonies. (5) Vague genre direction — the AI needs clear genre guidance. (6) No production quality tags — always add "Studio Mastered" or similar for polish.

What is tag stacking in Suno and how does it work?

+

Tag stacking is the technique of building a Suno style prompt using comma-separated keywords instead of full sentences. Rather than writing "a synthwave song with female vocals," you stack tags: "Synthwave, Ethereal Female Vocals." Each tag adds a layer of style direction. Key principles: lead with genre, mix 3-5 tags from different categories, use specific terms, avoid negation, and keep it under 200 characters. The most effective stacks combine Genre + Vibe + Vocals + Instruments + Production in that order.
